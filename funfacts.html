Fun Facts

Some fun facts about pastries!!!

Fact 1

·Baking dates back to ancient times, with the earliest known oven discovered in Croatia, dating back to 6500 BCE. Ancient Egyptians were among the first to perfect the art of baking bread, using yeast to make it rise and creating the first sourdough.

Fact 2

·Baking is essentially a chemistry experiment. The process involves precise measurements and reactions between ingredients. For example, when baking soda (a base) reacts with an acid like buttermilk or vinegar, it produces carbon dioxide gas, which helps baked goods rise and become fluffy.

Fact 3

·Eggs are incredibly versatile in baking. They act as a binder to hold ingredients together, a leavening agent to help products rise, and they also add moisture and richness to recipes. Their proteins help to create structure, while the yolks add flavor and color.

Fact 4

·Place sliced apples into the bottom crust, forming a slight mound. Lay four pastry strips vertically and evenly spaced over apples, using longer strips in the center and shorter strips at the edges.

Fact 5

·Butter is a crucial ingredient in many baked goods, providing flavour, moisture, and flakiness. When butter melts in the oven, it creates steam, which helps to lift and separate layers, resulting in light, flaky pastries and cookies.
